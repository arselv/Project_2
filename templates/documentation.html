Ride Alive

Creating Safer Riding Experiences via A.I.

Arsel Vargas, David Hutchinson, Maninder Bawa, Felipe Starling

Group 1 - Project 2 - Data Analytics

Our goal is to create a piece of software that, using artificial intelligence, will learn from existing motorcycle drivers to improve the riding experience of the current user. 

Our initial dataset will be limited in scope, to provide proof of concept and remove liability due to privacy laws. Once the software is created, new users will be able to willingly share their data, and therefore refine the learning database our software can rely upon. 

The first step in our plan, is to identify accident prone areas within a set location. This will allow our software to “Alert” users that they are approaching a dangerous area. 

Our second step is to analize the sex and age of people from our dataset, we plan to develop a marketing strategy that targets our “higher-risk” users, while also adding a higher “weight” to variables obtained from our “lower-risk” users. 

Our data will be displayed using a geographical visual, depicting the location of motorcycle accident incidents across the United Kingdom. We also plan to incorporate a “heatmap” layer, which should highlight higher incident areas than others. Further layers will allow the filtering of age and sex of those involved within accidents.
